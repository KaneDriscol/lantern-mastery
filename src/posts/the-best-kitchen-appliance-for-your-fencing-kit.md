I’m fully aware that finding time to train is difficult. Between work and family and everything else, the big open block of "go fence for an hour" doesn't show up as often as I'd like. My recommendation for anyone who is struggling with that same lack of time, start paying attention to the little moments that a scattered throughout your day. One of the best ones happens to be when your in the kitchen.

Waiting on your microwave.

## Why the Microwave Works

Think about it. It's an appliance you're almost guaranteed to use at least once a day, and every single time, it hands you a fixed, known amount of time and asks you to just stand there. A minute for oatmeal. Two for leftovers. Three for who knows what. That's not nothing. That's a **built-in timer** for a drill, sitting in your kitchen, that you were going to use anyway.

## First, Remember This

Before I get into what to actually do with that time, the more important point is this: **anything is better than nothing.** A few minutes a day, done consistently, beats a great hour-long session you only manage once a month. So don't let the shortness of the time talk you out of using it.

## What to Actually Do

So here's the idea. Next time you punch in your time and hit start, don't reach for your phone to scroll while you wait. Drop into a stance instead.

Hold it for the full minute or two. See how it feels to just sit there, weight settled, the way you would mid-bout. Then take your hand, stick two fingers out like you've got a blade in it, and go to work. Practice your blocks. Throw a few strikes. Run through an imaginary exchange against nobody, start to finish, using whatever's in your head from the last practice.

It's not a replacement for real steel and a real partner, and it's not going to teach you anything new on its own. But it *is* a minute or two of your body doing the thing, and that adds up.

## Make It Part of Your Kit

So next time you're wondering when you're going to fit training in, don't overthink it. You've already got a timer, and you're already standing there waiting. Make the microwave part of your fencing kit.
